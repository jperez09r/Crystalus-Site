I. The World

Meridian, and what surrounds it.

Beyond the walls of Meridian, the land shifts with the seasons — frozen peaks where something ancient stirs beneath the snow, burning badlands where survival is measured in water and steel, and forests that remember a time before the Empire drew its lines.

At the center stands Meridian — a city of order, power, and quiet control. Its rails carry more than people. Its walls hold back more than chaos.

But the balance is thinning. Resonance is rising. The grid is straining. And something buried deep beneath the capital has begun to listen.

II. The Four Doctrines

Four readings of the same truth.

Every faction believes it stands at the center of the world. Every faction is wrong in a different and beautiful way. These are the four readings of resonance, order, and the slow approach of the Pale Lycrux.

I · Imperial Doctrine

Harmony of the Empire

"Order is the only mercy. Contain first. Identify later."

Meridian's official creed. The Imperial doctrine stands for order, continuity, and civic harmony — a managed peace held in place by the Houses, the Rail Guard, and Harmony oversight. Discipline is a virtue. The grid is a covenant. Citizens are safe because the Empire is watching.

// Further reading sealed under Harmony directive.

II · Saona Doctrine

The Saona — The Vow of Resonance

"Listen first. Strike last. The world remembers what we do in silence."

A scattered order of scholar-monks bound by the Pilgrimage Vow. Saona doctrine stands for restraint, balance, and the old harmonics — the discipline of sensing resonance the way the Ancients did, before the Empire drew its grid. They train to protect, to steady, and to keep what is rising beneath the world from waking too soon.

// Saona canon is pre-Imperial. Records partial.

III · Ashlight Doctrine

Ashlight — The Burning Answer

"A throne held by silence is a throne already falling."

A doctrine of accelerated collapse. Ashlight stands for revolt, exposure, and the end of the Houses' monopoly — the conviction that the Empire's order is power dressed as safety. Their factions disagree on tactics; they agree the grid itself is the disease. Spectacle is their weapon. Panic is their proof.

// Marked Harmony Black. Existence officially denied.

IV · Broker Doctrine

The Broker — Leverage as Law

"Everything has a price. Faith is just a slow currency."

Not a faith — a market. The Broker doctrine stands for leverage, neutrality, and the law of exchange. They price what others worship: resonance, secrets, allegiance, silence. They do not need to understand the Crystalus to profit from it. The only truth is that someone, somewhere, will pay — and the Broker will know first.

// Sells to Empire, Saona, and Ashlight alike.

A bloodline that shouldn't exist.

III. Two Economies, One Empire

The Exchange — refined and raw.

Behind every empire is a ledger. Meridian's runs on resonance — refined Mint Glass currency, sealed and certified inside the capital, set against the raw, unstable shard barter of the outer villages. Both systems trade in the same material. Only one is allowed to call it currency.

// Capital Systems · Meridian

Mint Glass Economy — controlled flow.

Inside the walls of Meridian, value is sealed. Mint Glass is the only sanctioned currency — refined, harmonically imprinted, and stamped with mint authority. Five standard units run the city: Chip · Silver · Facet · Sharding · Piece.

Movement is permission. The Lantern Gate sorts every traveler by clearance tier; verification interfaces test resonance clarity, weight, and seal integrity. No barter. No exceptions. Refined Mint Glass shipments move only under sealed authority.

// Village Systems · Outer Reach

Raw Crystalus Economy — unrefined, unstable, unstandardized.

Beyond the rail line, the Empire's stamp loses its weight. Outer villages trade raw Crystalus shards directly — weighed against calibrated stones, priced by clarity and resonance, judged by trust and immediate need. There are no fixed prices. There are no sanctioned mints.

Shards are not just money out here. They are light, heat, fuel, tools, and clean water. A heat cradle warms a hall. A socketed shard lights a street. A rotating harness drives a smithy. The Empire calls it unregulated exchange. The villages call it survival.

IV. Crystalus Shards

Born from element. Imprinted by nature.

A Crystalus shard is not a tool. It is a fragment of negotiated Mako — alive with resonance, alive with memory. It chooses its bearer. The harness does not control it. The harness ensures the bearer does not die by it.

VI. M-Pilots — The Suit Forge Doctrine

Borrowed power, built to last.

Where bloodline failed, hardware answered. The M-Pilot discipline was forged in House Varric Dane — the first to argue that the Empire could not stake its survival on the lottery of who is born resonant. Their answer was the SentinelFrame: a suit that lets a non-wielder stand inside the same storm a Crystalus bearer walks through.

SF-01 · STANDARD ISSUE
// SUIT-FORGE SPEC SHEET · HOUSE VARRIC DANE · IMPERIAL RESTRICTED
// THE SENTINELFRAME CLASS

A suit that borrows what bloodlines were born with.

The SentinelFrame is the standard architecture for all current-line M-Pilot suits. A refined Crystalus core sits in a null-contact cell on the back; a Mako conversion matrix steps its resonance down into stable, phased energy; armor segments deploy in 0.8 to 1.2 seconds. The pilot does not wield. The suit does the wielding for them.

The Forge calls it borrowed power. The bloodline Houses call it heresy. The Empire calls it the Harvest — and signs the next contract.

// VARRIC DANE DOCTRINE

"Hardware wins. The future is not who you were born as — it's what you can be issued."

VII. Meridian Triumph

Strength through harmony.

The Empire's grand civic spectacle — and its most efficient laundering operation for resonance data, sponsorship leverage, and the quiet identification of new wielders. K-Levels are public. The standings are real. What happens after the trophy is awarded is not.

PROPAGANDA · CIVIC ORDER · SECTOR 18-C

Triumph is earned. Glory is yours.
The Empire watches.

Every sector runs qualifiers. Every qualifier ends in a K-Level reading. The Five Great Houses sponsor candidates, arbitrate brackets, and quietly take ownership of any wielder whose resonance signature catches the wrong eye. For the city, the Triumph is sport. For the Houses, it is selection.
